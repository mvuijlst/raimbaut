# Flagged Occitan readings for edition cross-check (corpus-v2 verse pages)

Compiled from the verse-transcription pass (2026). Every verse page was transcribed
FAITHFULLY from the 300-DPI image (no normalization). Most items below are legibly
printed but *unusual* archaic/manuscript forms — correctly transcribed, listed only
so they can be confirmed against Pattison (RO) / Rialto. A few are genuinely uncertain
glyphs (marked ⚠) that warrant an edition check.

## A. Genuinely uncertain glyphs (⚠ check against edition)
- **⚠ Chanson I & II — î vs ï diacritic** (v1p051 ll.22,25 `roîll`/`roîll'e`; v1p066 ll.15,16 `roîlha`/`roîl`): the mark over the *i* reads as a circumflex-like peak but may be a diaeresis (`roïll`). Occitan orthography favours the diaeresis. **Systematic — one decision fixes all four.**
    -> roïll
- **⚠ Chanson XXII** (v1p259 l.20 `[Neus]{.unclear}`): first word of the strophe partly obliterated by a scan smudge; read *Neus* from French "Et même". Confirm.
    -> Neus
- **⚠ Chanson XXXVI cont.** (v2p057, last verse line): a word between `qe·m` and `renei` is hand-struck-out and illegible — omitted, flagged `[[hand:]]`. Confirm nothing is lost.
    -> word was struck out, does not need to be mentioned or shown at all (*note that this is XXXIII, not XXXVI*)
- **⚠ Chanson XXXIX** (v2p096 l.8 `secx` — could be `secz`; l.14 `paro·m` interpunct faint).
    -> keep secx, paro·m
